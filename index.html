Active Galaxies:

Active galaxies are galaxies with extremely bright centres that produce huge amounts of energy. This happens because a supermassive black hole at the centre is pulling in gas, dust, and even stars. As this material falls towards the black hole, it becomes incredibly hot and releases large amounts of light and other forms of radiation. Active galaxies are special because they can be much brighter than all the stars in the rest of the galaxy combined.

Active galaxies can be spiral, elliptical, or irregular in shape, but what makes them different is their powerful core, called an active galactic nucleus (AGN). They are often hundreds of thousands of light-years across and can contain hundreds of billions of stars. Some active galaxies also produce enormous jets of energy that extend far into space.

One famous example of an active galaxy is Centaurus A, which has a bright centre powered by a supermassive black hole. Another example is Cygnus A, one of the strongest radio galaxies ever discovered. They are called "active galaxies" because their centres are constantly releasing huge amounts of energy. By studying active galaxies, astronomers can better understand black holes, galaxy evolution, and some of the most powerful events in the universe.
